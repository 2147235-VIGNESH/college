Message from Vice Chancellor

The research entails both reading and writing. These two skills help enable computation and comprehension. Without these skills, it is less likely for anyone to appreciate and get involved in research. Reading opens the mind to a vast horizon of knowledge, while writing helps a reader use her/his own perspective and transform this into a more concrete idea that s/he understands. Apart from reading and writing, listening and speaking are also central in conducting research. Interviews with experts, attending knowledge-generating events like webinars, MOOCs, TED Talks all aid in formulating research topics. They can also facilitate the critical thinking process. While I leave you with these suggestions, I want you to know that “Research is to see what everybody else has seen and to think what nobody else has thought” (Albert Szent-Györgyi). I wish you all success Fr Dr Abraham VM Vice Chancellor
